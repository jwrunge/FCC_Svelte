HEART OF THE MATTER
OPEN THE EYES OF MY HEART

   by Pastor Martha-Jean Fitch
May 24, 2020
TEXT:  Luke 24:44-53, Ephesians 1:15-23

 

            In fourth grade, I clearly remember the day when the school nurse came into the class to take students down to her office to do a vision screening.   When I got there, I really couldn’t read anything on that chart, other than maybe the big “E”.   So, I was given a letter to take home to my parents.   I remember thinking I was in trouble…that I had done something wrong.   You see I had always sat on the front row in classrooms and never complained about not being able to see.   I didn’t think I was missing anything.

 

            Well, my parents took me to the eye doctor and discovered I was very near-sighted…so I had to get a prescription for glasses.  I remember so clearly the day that my mother took me to the glasses store and we picked up my first pair of glasses.   We walked out of the store and as we walked to the car, I looked across the street and saw a grove of trees.   And I said to my mother – “Oh my goodness!   Those trees have leaves on them!”    I was so amazed.   For the longest time,  I think my eyes had told me that trees were just bushy without any details.      It was a delightful surprise to see leaves and branches!  My eyes were opened to new world!

 

            You know, God can surprise us in other ways – not just with correcting our physical eyesight – but also our spiritual eyesight …through the work of the Holy Spirit.   That’s certainly what happened to the disciples throughout those days after Easter.    You will remember about three weeks ago, we read those two disciples that went on the road to Emmaus.   They were sad and discouraged because they had seen Jesus die on the cross.  Their eyes were kept from seeing the risen Lord who joined them along the road.   Jesus started to explain to them all about the Scripture and about how they all pointed to the Messiah.   And then, when they finally reached their home, Jesus joined them in their home and sat down for a meal with them.   Jesus took the bread and blessed it and broke it and gave it to them.   Suddenly their eyes were opened, and they knew Jesus was alive!

 

With great excitement and joy, they ran all the way back to Jerusalem to tell the other disciples.   As they were telling the story to the disciples, all of the sudden, Jesus was standing among them!   They were afraid at first – thinking they were seeing a ghost.   But Jesus proved He wasn’t a ghost by showing them His hands and feet – and He even ate some broiled fish as they watched!   

 

            And then Jesus opened their eyes to help them see better and understand clearly who He was as the risen Lord.    Let’s hear what He tells them in our Scripture reading for today:

 

Jake reads Luke 24:44-53

 

            Jesus opened up their minds to understand the Scriptures.   As I read this Scripture – I kind of envision a “light bulb” going on in the mind of the disciples.   Where they were confused before, and had poor vision – now all the sudden (just like me finally seeing the leaves on the trees with my new glasses) they could finally see and understand.   “Oh – that’s what that meant!   Oh – I can see that connection there – and that’s why that happened!  Jesus really is the Messiah!”   The disciples were finally seeing with new eyes and new understandings.    And with this new faith and understanding, they were empowered then to go out and be witnesses for Christ and build the church.

 

              You know, the Holy Spirit can open our minds for our understanding as well.   Have you ever had one of those times when you are reading a passage in the Bible – or  sitting in a Bible Study  - or even in worship, and all of the sudden you have that “light bulb” experience, like the disciples did?   You suddenly see what the passage means – or how it speaks directly to your life.    That is the Holy Spirit opening you up to see in a deeper way – to gain a new perspective – and to prepare you for action.  

 

            Earlier, we sang the words:   “Open my eyes that I may see, glimpses of truth You have for me.”   That really should be our prayer each time we come to study God’s word.   We can simply pray “Open the eyes of my heart God – help me to understand this passage and what it means for my life.”    Claim that promise that just as the Holy Spirit opened up the mind of the Disciples – He can guide you and direct you in your understanding.

 

            The Apostle Paul prayed for the early Christians to have open hearts as well – to have the knowledge and wisdom of God and really knew God’s strength and power.   Let’s hear that prayer in our next Scripture reading:

 

Jake reads Ephesians 1:15-23

 

            Paul’s great prayer for the church is that they would know God better – that they would know God is a deep and personal way.    He prayed it would be more than just going through the motions but would be a vital, growing deep relationship, filled with the wisdom and insight from God.

 

            And he prays specifically that “the eyes of your heart may be enlightened” – or other translations say, “that the eyes of your heart be flooded with light.”   The Message version says, “I ask the God of our Master, Jesus Christ, the God of glory—to make you intelligent and discerning in knowing Him personally, your eyes focused and clear, so that you can see.”   Oh, how our eyes of our spiritual hearts can be fuzzy and out of focus – falling to the lies of the world around us – and to the lies of the evil one.   We can fall into darkness, thinking there is no hope – that we have no strength or help.   Our poor vision can also cause us to make poor decisions and judgements… and can even cause us to not see the neighbor we have in Christ.   We end up fighting and attacking and dividing rather than seeing them as our neighbors we are called to love.   And during times like we are in right now, our eyes can be clouded with uncertainty and confusion – and we can’t always see clearly the way ahead.   We really need the Holy Spirit to come and flood our hearts with His light and sharpen our focus and open our minds with understanding and wisdom.   To give us discernment on how to best live as His children in this troubled world.

 

            And so, we pray along with the Apostle Paul, that we our eyes be opened to the confident hope He has given us.    We can have confident hope that God is trustworthy and faithful and will keep His promises.   There is never a time when we are alone - never a situation that can separate us from God.   When our poor vision says that we can’t make it – that circumstances of life are too difficult – we can pray for that clearer vision of God, pointing us to that confident hope that even in the midst of the struggles, God is there and will carry us through.

 

            And we also pray, along with the Apostle Paul, that our eyes will be opened to the rich and glorious blessings that we have in Christ.   We are bombarded it seems by “bad news” these days… and sometimes our eyes can be pretty pessimistic and negative.   We need our eyes flooded with the light of God and see the amazing blessings we have and focus more on the promises of God.  Maybe a challenge for all of us, as we watch the news – and read social media – would be to pray, “Open my eyes Lord to see the blessings and the good.   Open my eyes to gratitude and kindness.   And help me to share that good news.”

 

            Finally, the Apostle Paul prays for us to have our eyes opened to something really amazing – that the same mighty power that raised Jesus from the dead – is available to you and to me!   I don’t know about you, but there are times when I can feel pretty weak or powerless.   Life can be tough – and stressful.   It would be easy to let my eyes be filled with discouragement or even depression.   But the good news is that we have God’s mighty power living in us.   As we call out to God – we will find that God’s power is sufficient for all we need.   He will give us extra-ordinary strength to endure – and the ability to do what you may think is impossible – and can even resurrect what be dead and dying in your life.  That is an incredible promise to claim – to get us through life each day.

 

            You know, I am so glad that I got glasses back when I was 9 years old.   A new world opened up for me with that corrected vision.   And now I can’t live without that correction – it literally helps me see to get through life!    That same year that I got my glasses, I went forward in church and gave my life to Christ.   I was baptized and I really think God opened the eyes of my heart – gave me a new vision to see and get through life.   His Spirit moves in my heart and gives me confident hope – rich and glorious blessings – and an incredible power to keep me going each day!

 

            I pray that you too can know that hope and that power!  

 

            May God open our eyes that we can see His face.   May God open our ears that we can hear His voice.   May God open our hearts that we might love like He does.

 

GOODWILL MOMENT

 

 What does it mean to look through the eyes of our hearts? How do we see things around us in a heightened way? When our level of love goes up, our level of appreciation and gratitude goes up as well. I invite you to look around you. What do you see in front of you that may be something or someone you see every day but could see with new eyes of gratitude?  Pause for a moment think about at least three things or people in this moment.

What did you just see when you looked around and did looking at it with “the eyes of a grateful heart” offer you a new perspective?

 

What else are you grateful for at this moment?

 

            To be the “body of Christ” as the church is to see the world through the eyes of Jesus–to see through the eyes of love. As Christ’s body here on earth, we are called to create the same conditions of love that He did while He was here on earth. We hope that “the eyes of our hearts” can be continually opened, as the disciples’ were that day of Jesus’ ascension, so that we can be the best witnesses of His love here on earth.   

 

            What can you do this week, with opened hearts, to reach out and show gratitude and love to someone?   How can you uplift someone with the good news of the confident hope we have in Jesus and the amazing gift of resurrection power?

 

             You can start by sharing in the comment section below how God is opening the eyes of your heart.   Be a witness for how God is at work in your life.   And then – take a moment to reach out to a neighbor or friend and express your gratitude and give them a message of hope.
