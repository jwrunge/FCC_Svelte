THE HEART OF THE MATTER
NO TROUBLE IN MY HEART

    by Pastor Martha-Jean Fitch

MAY 10, 2020
TEXT:  John 14:1-7, 27-29

 

 

            There’s a story about a little boy who was afraid of the dark.   One night his mother told him to go out on the back porch and bring her the broom. The little boy turned to his mother and said, "Mama, I don't want to go out there. It's dark." The mother smiled reassuringly at her son. "You don't have to be afraid of the dark," she explained. "Jesus is out there. He'll look after you and protect you." The little boy looked at his mother real hard and asked, "Are you sure He's out there?" "Yes, I'm sure. He is everywhere, and He is always ready to help you when you need Him," she said. The little boy thought about that for a minute, then went to the back door and cracked it a little.  Peering out into the darkness, he called, "Jesus? If you're out there, would you please hand me that broom?"

 

Is your heart sometimes troubled about something? Do you feel scared sometimes? There are so many uncertainties in life, especially in these days of the pandemic, that we can hardly avoid feeling scared sometimes, and letting our troubles weigh us down.

 

Troubles come in all shapes and sizes and to all people, both young and old.    They can be small annoyances and frustrations to great adversity and devastating trials.   Jesus said that in this world we would have tribulation – or troubles.   There is no such thing as a trouble-free life.   The big question is how we handle our troubles.   And who we will trust in the midst of our troubles.

 

Jesus Himself was troubled at times.   At the Garden of Gethsemane, for example, He said His soul was crushed with grief to the point of death.   But He went to His heavenly Father in prayer for help and strength to be able to do His Father’s will.

 

What do you do when you are facing troubles in life?   Can you find peace in the midst of the trials?   Can you face troubles in life without becoming overwhelmed and anxious and crippled with fear?

 

Our Scripture reading today is from the Gospel of John, the 14th chapter.   It comes when the disciples are in the upper room with Jesus, on that night before Jesus went to the cross for us.   Jesus has just told them that one of them was to betray Him – and that Peter was going to deny Him.   And then – worst of all – that He was going to be leaving them.   Their Master and the Lord – the one they had walked with very closely for three years – the one they loved so very much – leaving them!   How could his be?   It didn’t make sense at all.  They were heartbroken and very troubled.

 

Let’s hear how Jesus responds and reassures them.

 

(Kim Barton reads John 14:1-7, 27-29)

 

Thanks be to God for these words of our Lord.

 

When Jesus saw how troubled and upset the disciples were, He wanted to assure them and help them with their fearful and anxious thoughts.   Just like a mother, who calms a fearful child – Jesus soothes their fears and encourages them with these words, “Let not your hearts be troubled” – “Peace I give you”.    “Don’t be afraid or troubled”.   “Trust me.”

 

Many of us have been blessed and comforted by these words that describe that Jesus is preparing a place in heaven for us – and that when everything is ready, Jesus will come to get us so that where He is, there we will be also.

 

But I think these words are meant for life on this earth as well – before we die.   Jesus gives us comfort and encouragement that He will be there with us, wherever we go.   With the gift of the Spirit of the God living in us, there is no longer any reason to let our hearts be troubled.  The peace that the Holy Spirit gives is not like that of the world’s peace – which is usually defined as the absence of trouble or conflict.  No, the peace of the Holy Spirit has nothing to do with what our external circumstances are – but everything to do with what is going on, on the inside.   Peace is being calm when everything is falling apart – when your world is rocking around you – knowing that the Holy Spirit gives you that invisible means of support.   He can work in you to give you peace of mind and heart and give you hope and strength to carry on.

 

So, when troubles come your way – trust and rely on the Holy Spirit, that is living inside of you.   Call on Him when you are anxious and ask for His peace that passes all understanding.   Jesus said that the Holy Spirit would guide us into all truth and remind us about all Jesus taught.   When we open ourselves up to the work of the Holy Spirit in our lives, we will be filled up with words of Jesus that will encourage and comfort, even in the midst of the troubles going on around us.

 

When we find ourselves being overwhelmed by our troubles and filled with anxiety, we need to turn those worries into prayers.   The Apostle Paul encouraged us in his letter to the Philippians not to have any anxiety but commit ourselves to prayer – and the peace that passes all understanding would be ours.  

 

            Pastor and writer Greg Laurie says, “The next time you are gripped by fear and worry, turn it into a prayer like this one:  ‘Lord I don’t know what to do, but I trust You.   You are in control, and I commit this to You right now.’” 1 You may need to pray that prayer again and again as you work through your anxiety – but you can be assured that God wants to fill You with His peace – and help carry you through those troubling times.   He can do that as you look to Him and trust Him to do what you can’t.

 

            And claim the promises of God.   Instead of counting all the things you are anxious or fearful of – count the promises of God.   Recount the ways He has been faithful to you in the past.   And know that He has Your future in His hands.   Hold on to those promises throughout Scripture – memorize them so that you can call on them at any point.  The words of God have power – and they can defeat the words of the enemy that are trying to consume you with fear and anxiety.   God’s words bring hope and peace – and He always keeps His promises.

 

            And most of all – trust Him.   As it said in our Scripture:   “Trust in God, and trust also in me.   I am the way, the truth and the life.”   Right in the midst of our uncertain and frightening times, we can remind ourselves again and again of these words:   “I will put my trust in God and not be afraid.”

 

            There was a little boy flying on a plane – and the plane got into some massive turbulence.   He was sitting next to a lady who was absolutely terrified.   She was scared to death and trembling in fear.   The boy, on the other hand seemed quite calm as he continued to play with his toy.   The women said, “Boy, how can you be so calm when you are in this much trouble?”   He said, “The reason I can be this calm is because my Daddy is the pilot”.

 

            In this life – you’re going to have some turbulence and trouble. But when you know who is in control – You know that He is able and faithful to get you through whatever you have to face.  

 

            Do you know who’s in control?   Is Jesus your pilot in your life that can steer you through the stormy seas and turbulent windstorms?   Call on Him now, cast on Him your every care.   Feel safe and secure in loving arms of our Savior.2

 

GOODWILL MOMENT

 

            Our theme scripture says of the early Christians, “they ate their food with glad and generous hearts.” One way we can be glad and generous is to share about how we are finding strength, hope, love and peace in these days. This is part of “breaking bread” with each other as we break open our hearts to one another as well. In this week’s scripture, we are invited to trust in God, the rock, and to take a break from worry.    

 

           Are you finding it difficult to let go of worry, to take a break?

 

What have you witnessed this week that helped you feel an ongoing and steadfast love that was comforting?

 

 Or if you can’t think of something from this week, what do you have in your memory as something or someone that offered comfort?

 

Take some time to reflect on those questions and be sure and write down your answers – so you can remember it.   Matter of fact – how about writing a few words on the comments below.   Let others know how you find comfort in times of trouble.  

 

 

 

 

 

BENEDICTION  

 

As we close this time together, remember:

God is always with you. No matter what you face,

no matter what trials or hardships come your way,

God is right beside you, ready to hold you, to be a rock for you,

guiding and directing your path.

Take heart! This is the heart of the matter.

Amen.
