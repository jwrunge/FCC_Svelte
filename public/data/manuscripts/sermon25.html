HEART OF THE MATTER
HEALER OF BROKEN HEARTS

    

By Pastor Martha Jean Fitch
April 26, 2020
TEXT:  Luke 24:13-35

 

 

 

            The Heart of the Matter.   That is our topic for Eastertide this year. Last week, Pastor Greg talked about all the hearts he saw as he drove around – hearts posted everywhere, on windows and doors. And then – did you all see the great article in the Knox County Neighbors newspaper about Galesburg’s own Krista Wynes who created the Heart Hunters back in March? Krista envisioned the idea of the hearts as a way to reach out to neighbors while staying home and social distancing. Her hope was to spread kindness and bring people together. And incredibly, her idea spread like wildfire. People throughout all 50 states and on all seven continents and in 96 countries are putting these hearts up in their windows and doors!!   And Krista was even commended in a recent meeting of the United States House of Representatives! Who knew such a simple act would make such a powerful impact!

 

            Those hearts seem to me, to bring hope – and express love – and unity… that we are all in this together – literally all around the world!

 

            As I have reflected on those hearts throughout this week…and as I have watched the news about COVID 19 – I kept thinking, you know a lot of those hearts really could be broken hearts. I am so amazed to see that in our country, we now have over 50,000 deaths from the coronavirus – and the numbers of cases in Illinois continue to climb. Broken hearts as people are ill – as people grieve. And then this past week, to hear that we will not be able to be gather for worship in our sanctuary for at least another 5 weeks! Broken-hearted – I must admit. I know I am truly missing all of you!

 

            But I am continually claiming the verse from Psalm 34:18 – “The Lord is close to the brokenhearted; He rescues those whose spirits are crushed.”  Let me say that one more time – it’s a great verse to remember – especially when we are walking through these difficult times:   “The Lord is close to the brokenhearted; He rescues those whose spirits are crushed.” 

 

            A disciple named Cleopas and his companion discovered the power of this verse as they met the risen Lord on the road to Emmaus on that first Easter. They were walking on that road, broken hearted, with spirits crushed – and suddenly the risen Lord walked among them. Let’s hear that story right now.

 

LESLIE WILT READS THE SCRIPTURE

 

            Thanks Leslie! And thanks be to God for those words.

 

            Think about these two followers of Jesus. They were walking down the road to Emmaus, a little village 7 miles west of Jerusalem. They were going home in defeat, shame, and disappointment. It was late in the evening and the sinking sun reminded them of the sinking feeling they had on the inside. They had faced the sunset of Good Friday, watching Jesus die on the cross and they felt all hope was gone. They were walking along a dark and lonely path, brokenhearted.

 

            But as they walked along, a stranger joined them. He asked what they had been discussing. They stopped dead in their tracks, looked at each other and then asked him if he was the only person who did not know what had been going on the last few days. He asked them what things they were talking about. They said, “about Jesus of Nazareth, a mighty prophet and how he had been put to death.” Then they said, "But we had hoped he was the one to redeem Israel."                  

 

"We had hoped" -- what sad and tragic words. These two followers had lost all their hope. Their hope had literally been buried in Jerusalem! But things soon changed. God would take their dashed hopes and dreams and give them new life. Because of their visit with this stranger, they would never be the same again.

 

            The stranger walked with them all the way to Emmaus, teaching them all about the scriptures and prophecies. He told them so much and they wanted to hear more. So, they invited him to come and stay with them. They sat down at the table for a meal together. He blessed the bread and broke it. Then he gave it to them. There was something strangely familiar in all of that. And then Luke writes, "Their eyes were opened, and they recognized him."    Suddenly everything was different. The entire situation was transformed. Nothing would ever be the same again. After Jesus disappeared from them – they said, “Didn’t our hearts burn within us as He talked with us on the road and explained the Scriptures to us?”  They had gone from having hearts that were broken to hearts burning with great joy and hope. And within the hour, the disciples turned around and ran to Jerusalem and told everyone the good news: “The Lord has risen indeed!”

 

            How often we are just like those followers. We are overcome by grief and anxiety. We are so worried about what our future holds and keep our eyes focused on the negative. We are tempted to angry or depressed – or just give up; thinking nothing is ever going to go right.

 

            But the good news is that we have a traveler who walks along beside us – someone who understands what it means to suffer. Someone who understands what it means to lose a loved one in death and what it means to struggle with God’s will for your life.   Someone who understands what it means to be beaten and what it means to face death. That traveler is Jesus who is walking beside us, every step of the way. And when we take the time to open our eyes and  look for Him in the midst of our experiences, He can give us the power and the strength to turn around and get us on the right road that leads to the sunrise.

 

We can get on that right road that leads to joy and hope, when we remember that Christ is with us, guiding us and never, ever leaving us!  Jesus said, “I am with you always!”   He is alive and will never abandon us!

 

            When our thinking has been gripped by that conviction, then we can face anything that comes along. When our hearts burn within us with the conviction that Jesus is with us and will give us power, we know we can handle all of life, its ups, and its downs.

            Once during a time when Martin Luther was greatly troubled, some of his friends saw him writing with his finger in the dust on the table, "He lives. He lives."  Maybe that is what we should do with those hearts – write the words: "He lives. He lives!”   Because He lives, I can face tomorrow!    And then maybe, just maybe, our broken hearts will begin to be healed! And maybe, during this time,  our eyes will be opened and we’ll see how our Redeemer is walking among us – and hear His voice – and we’ll know – we’ll never be the same!
