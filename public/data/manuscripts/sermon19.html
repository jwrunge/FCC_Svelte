WHO ARE YOU
 REMEMBER YOUR NAME

 

 

  By Pastor Martha-Jean Fitch
February 2, 2020
TEXT:   Psalm 139, John 1:40-51

 

 

 

            When I was a young child, one of my favorite shows was called “Romper Room”.    Anybody my age or older remember that show?   Romper Room was a show that was often called a televised kindergarten of sorts.   The hostess of the show – I remember her called Miss Nancy – would begin the show with the Pledge of Allegiance.  Throughout the show, children on would do different activities and would learn how to behave.   Kids were encouraged to be a “Do Bee” and do things that were right and not be a “Don’t Bee”.   Kids were even served milk and cookies during the show and before eating, would always say the Romper Room prayer: “God is great, God is good.   Let us thank Him for our food.   AMEN.”   At the end of each show, Miss Nancy would look through a “magic mirror” and then she’d say these words: “Romper, stomper, bomper boo.   Tell me, tell me, tell me, do.   Magic Mirror, tell me today, did all my friends have fun at play.   I see Kathryn and Jennifer, Johnny and Mark.”1   And she’d say all sorts of names.   And I would always wait with great anticipation that one day she would say “Martha-Jean”.   Now I heard her say Martha a few times, and maybe even a girl named Jean.   But those weren’t my name.   No – I am Martha-Jean and that’s what I was wanting to hear.   But I never, ever got to hear Miss Nancy say my name.   I wanted her to see me and know me!   It would have meant the world to me to hear Miss Nancy say my name!  

 

            There is something powerful about hearing our name being mentioned.   It gives us our identity and shows relationship.   Dale Carnegie taught people to “Remember that a person’s name is to that person the sweetest and most important sound in any language.”2   I have always gone by the name Martha-Jean, but in college, my name was shortened to “MJ” because, one of my professors, said, “Martha-Jean” was just too long.   And “MJ” stuck.   But now if I hear someone call me “Martha-Jean” I know that person has known me for a long time – and probably knows me very well.  

 

            What is so amazing is that God knows each and everyone of our names – and knows us very well!   In fact, our name is on His lips and engraved upon His palms.    And as He looks out at His creation – not through a magic mirror like Miss Nancy – but through His eyes of love that are always seeing and knowing – He will call and won’t miss one single name!

 

            Simon and Nathanael discovered Jesus calling their names in our story for today from John 1.   Open your Bible to John 1 and we are going to read verses 40-51.     Here begins the reading:

 

40 Andrew, Simon Peter’s brother, was one of these men who heard what John said and then followed Jesus. 41 Andrew went to find his brother, Simon, and told him, “We have found the Messiah” (which means “Christ”). 42 Then Andrew brought Simon to meet Jesus. Looking intently at Simon, Jesus said, “Your name is Simon, son of John—but you will be called Cephas” (which means “Peter”).

            Jesus looked intently at Simon.   I kind of imagine that He was looking deeply into Simon’s soul and saw something great there.  The new name Jesus gives him was Cephas in Aramaic or Peter in Greek – and they both mean “Rock”.   Simon would truly become the “Rock” upon which the early church grew.   Jesus knew Simon – who he was and who he would become.

 

            And what was true for Simon Peter – is true for us as well.   God not only knows us but knows all we can become when we place our lives into His hands.  

 

            Let’s continue our story and find out what happens to Nathanael.  Look at verse 43.

 

43 The next day Jesus decided to go to Galilee. He found Philip and said to him, “Come, follow me.” 44 Philip was from Bethsaida, Andrew and Peter’s hometown.

45 Philip went to look for Nathanael and told him, “We have found the very person Moses and the prophets wrote about! His name is Jesus, the son of Joseph from Nazareth.”

46 “Nazareth!” exclaimed Nathanael. “Can anything good come from Nazareth?”

“Come and see for yourself,” Philip replied.

47 As they approached, Jesus said, “Now here is a genuine son of Israel—a man of complete integrity.” 48 “How do you know about me?” Nathanael asked.

Jesus replied, “I could see you under the fig tree before Philip found you.”

49 Then Nathanael exclaimed, “Rabbi, you are the Son of God—the King of Israel!”

50 Jesus asked him, “Do you believe this just because I told you I had seen you under the fig tree? You will see greater things than this.” 51 Then he said, “I tell you the truth, you will all see heaven open and the angels of God going up and down on the Son of Man, the one who is the stairway between heaven and earth.”

 

            Jesus knew Nathanael – even though the two of them had never met.  He could see Nathanael at a distance…and He knew what kind of man he was – “a man of complete integrity”.   He knew he would be one of His disciples.

 

            What is amazing about these two stories is that Jesus not only saw and knew Simon and Nathanael in a deep and personal way – He sees and knows us and is calling us to be His disciples.

 

             In Isaiah 43:1-2, we hear God say to Israel– and to us: “O Israel, the one who formed you says, “Do not be afraid, for I have ransomed you.   I have called you by name, you are mine!”  And with Jesus as our Good Shepherd, “He calls His own sheep by name and leads them out.”  The Shepherd walks ahead of the sheep and they follow Him because they know His voice.”  (John 10:13) 

 

            Isn’t that incredible!  God has called us by name – He knows each and every one of us completely.   We are living in the age when we are known by numbers – our account numbers, our Social Security numbers, our driver’s license numbers.  We are kept account of through those numbers coded into some corporate computer somewhere.  And when you need to talk to someone about some issue in your life, so often you call the company and all you get is a computer voice rather than an actual person.   And the computer doesn’t call you by name or show you any compassion and understanding.   But God – who is the Creator of the universe and of billions of people, knows you personally by name – and calls you His own.   I hope you get a sense of how absolutely amazing that is!   We have a personal God who takes notice of each and everyone of us – because of His great love for us.                 

 

         During the last census, a mother was asked by a census taker how many children she had.   She had a very large family, so she started to go through all her children, “Oh, let’s see now, there’s Billy, and there’s Harry, and there’s Martha, and there’s….”   And the census taker got a little impatient and annoyed with her, ‘Never mind their names, just give me the number!”   Well the mother got very indignant and said to the man, “They don’t have numbers, they all have names!”

 

            That’s the way it is with our relationship with the Lord.   God knows our names and knows everything about us.   He knows us completely and thoroughly – and delights in us.   In fact, God says you are His masterpiece (Ephesians 2:10) and you are precious and honored by God.   It doesn’t matter what circumstances are saying to you – or how others have defined you in the past – God treasures you and calls you a friend! (Romans 5:11)   He knows everything about us – even to knowing how sinful we are – and was willing to die for us, because of His great love for us.   (Romans 5:8)

 

            King David was incredibly amazed at how God knows everything about us, when he wrote Psalm 139.   Instead of me just reading it to you, right now, we are going to read a paraphrase of the psalm together responsively.   The words will be up on the screen.   As we read, I want you to try and make this a prayer to God – a prayer of thanksgiving for how God knows you and loves you:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  

O Lord, you have searched me and known me.

You know when I’m sitting around and when I’m up and about.

From a distance, you know what I am thinking.

You search for me wherever I go.

You make my life your concern.

 

Before a thought has formed in my mind - long before it makes it to my mouth – you already know about it.

I am surrounded by your protection.

I am aware that your hand is upon me.

This gives me a great sense of security.

 

Knowing all this is too wonderful for me!

My little mind cannot contain it!

 

Where can I go from your spirit?

Where can I go that you are not there?

When my spirit soars, I find you are there.

When I am really down in the dumps, I find you there as well.

If my morning starts off perfectly, your hand is there to lead me.

If I find my place halfway around the world, your right hand is there to hold tightly to me.

 

Sometimes I say, “Surely I will be overwhelmed by the darkness I encounter,”

 I realize that even darkness doesn’t affect you.

When I think, “The daylight around me is becoming nighttime,” I find that the night becomes as bright as day. To you, even the darkness is light.

 

It was you, O Lord, who formed me from the inside out.

In my mother’s womb, you were there knitting me together.

I am fearfully and wonderfully made, and I praise you for that.

This I know very well: you do good work.

 

When I was being made in secret – intricately woven together in those depths - my framework was in full-sight to you, even as there was no form or shape to my substance.

My days were written down in your journal, even before I began living out my life.

 

The way you think is way beyond the way I think.

The sum of your thoughts is way beyond my understanding.

I have tried to count them, but they are more than the sand.

When I come to the end of my trying to figure you out, I am amazed - because I am still with you.

 

Search me, O God.

Know the deepest part of who I am.

Put me to the kind of test that will prove who I really am.

I want you to know my thoughts and find them acceptable.

Check for any wicked way that permeates me.

In the end, lead me in your everlasting pathway.

 

            God knows you and loves you.   He knows you through and through – from the very moment you were conceived.   He knows your ups and downs - the times when you are faithful and the times when you sin and fall away.  And He still is reaching out to you – calling your name and wanting to be in a close, personal relationship with you.

 

            The problem many of us have is that we have let others define who we are.  Instead of filling ourselves up with the precious names God gives us – we hang on to labels and names that over the years have come become a part of us.   Some of them may have come from other folks – hurtful nicknames or judgmental comments.   Maybe names like loser or slow – fat or ugly.   And you feel like you are inadequate or not good enough.   Others names you may have internalized because of the situation you have been in.  For example, you may have been around cliques that didn’t want to include you, so internally you took on the name – unwanted or unloved.   Maybe you’ve had a great loss and your heart was hurt and so, you took on the name hopeless and broken.   Or that horrible thing you did keeps rearing its ugly head and calling you names like unforgiveable or worthless.   Those memories and those names can get buried in the crevices of our minds and sometimes we can feel totally trapped and in bondage to that way of thinking.   These names are lies that Satan wants you to believe!   This is not the identity God gave you.   That is not who God says you are.

            God sent Jesus to break the bondage that we have to Satan’s lies.   Christ comes into our lives and transforms us and makes us into new creations.   Through His love and His grace, He tells us that we are unconditionally loved, accepted and His precious child.   Those are the names we need to claim over our lives.

 

            I have asked the deacons to pass out to you, now, some bookmarks that have some names on them – and some affirmations about who you are in Christ.   When you get them – I want you to look on the side that says, “My New Name”.  It says, “Thank you Father God, that I am” and then there is a blank you can fill in with all those names that are listed there.   I am beloved, chosen, precious, provided for, complete, welcomed….and so on.   These are just some of the names that Scripture says about who you are.  

 

            This is the truth that we need to live by.   Instead of living with the lies of this world – we need to program our minds to hear the truth of God.   Right when we are tempted to take on that false name – we need to turn to God’s word and remind ourselves who we are in Christ.   Filling ourselves up with His promises and His names for us is the great weapon of truth that will fight against the lies that are so easy to come by. 

 

            So - “The next time your heart aches, and you start to feel lonely, rejected or you start to doubt your worth, pause and think about this powerful truth:   Jesus knows and calls you by your name!”3   You are His beloved child  - accepted, forgiven, loved!   That is who you are!   Remember your name!
