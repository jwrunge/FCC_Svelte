WHO ARE YOU
LED BY A STAR

 

  By Pastor Martha-Jean Fitch

January 5, 2020
TEXT:   Isaiah 60:1-2, Matthew 2:1-12

 

 

 

            Happy New Year to all of you!   It is good to see you in this new

decade – and new year!  I trust you all had a wonderful holiday!  I know many of you were ill over the holidays – so I am glad you are feeling better.   And together we have all grieved the loss of 4 of our church members since December 18.   Our loss is definitely their glory in their heavenly home with our Lord.   I pray all of you will be able to join in the celebration of Joan Shawgo’s life and faith this coming Saturday, January 11 at 11am, here in our sanctuary.

 

            The beginning of a new calendar year – and often, even at a time of a death - we stop and reflect on the year past and look toward to the coming year – and ask who we are and where we are going in life.    In the midst of a death like some of the saints of the church, we might look at their life and then ask what of their life and example can impact our lives.

 

            So, for the next few weeks, we are going to take a deep journey into the question, “Who are you?”   What amazing things could happen if we lived our best, most passionate selves – led by the Holy Spirit – in the coming year?

 

            A little over 20 years ago, I was getting ready to write my new year’s sermon for our church in Texas and I had the most amazing dream.   Many of you know, I sometimes have very vivid dreams and some of them are quite memorable.   This one I had that new year of 1998 was one of those, without a doubt.   While it was a little strange, I think it was full of all sorts of meaning.

 

            In my dream, the church family was at an outdoor amphitheater for what appeared to be a worship service.   The congregation was sitting on concrete benches and Greg and I were sitting up front, facing everyone, as if we were preparing to lead worship.   It was nighttime and I was looking up at the sky and admiring the beauty of the stars.   And then it happened, the most amazing event.

 

             It all began with a bright, shining star.   I noticed it and quickly told Greg to look at it.  Pretty soon, this bright light changed into what looked like a spaceship – or a small shuttle perhaps.   That spaceship flew straight up and connected with another spaceship and both shined brightly.   Pretty soon, these two ships flew over us and connected with a third spaceship and then hovered right over us!    Now these three space- ships were in some way connected together and shone like a huge bright star.   As we watched this magnificent sight, all of the sudden smoke started billowing out of the spaceships and soon we heard a deep, man’s voice talking – from the sky!   The voice said: “I want you to live at peace with each other.   Take care of each other, love each other and follow my commandments.”

 

            After that, the three spaceships flew back over us and became one shining bright star again.   As I watched, I turned to Greg and said, “That’s the brightest light I have ever seen.”   Soon, that light changed to a cloud.   And that cloud was in the shape of a baby – and it looked just like baby Jesus!    As we watched, we saw the baby reach out his arms – and then the image disappeared.   And with that the sky was back to normal.

 

            Well, after we had seen such a wonderful vision, everyone dropped to their knees and started praising God.   And I began to sing, “Praise God from Whom all blessings flow” …when my alarm clock rang, and I woke up!    Believe me, I did not want that dream to end that day!

            Now before you write me off as totally crazy – think about the dream I had.   Bright lights in the sky – a baby reaching out to us – the message:   Live at peace – follow my commandments.   Sounds like Epiphany to me!

 

            Epiphany - that season that follows Christmas.   Epiphany comes at the end of the twelve days of Christmas and officially begins January 6.   The word “epiphany” literally means to be manifest or to appear openly.   We begin the season of Epiphany hearing the story of the wise men seeing the star in the sky and following it to see the Son of God revealed in a baby born in Bethlehem.  The good news of Epiphany is that a star has shone and has brought light in the midst of darkness. That star revealed to us that Jesus is the Savior of the entire world – not just for the Jews.

 

            For me, Epiphany is that time for us to wonder what we will do now that we have been to the stable.   We have been given the light of Christ.   What are we going to do with that light?   Are we going to let our light shine in 2020?   The wise men were definitely changed by seeing the star that led them to Jesus.   And my guess is that they reflected that light to all they met, as they went home by a different path.

 

            Let’s remind ourselves of their story, turning in your Bibles to Matthew 2 and looking at verses 1-12.   Matthew – that very first book of the New Testament. The story begins by saying:

 

1Jesus was born in Bethlehem in Judea, during the reign of King Herod. About that time some wise men from eastern lands arrived in Jerusalem, asking, 2 “Where is the newborn king of the Jews? We saw his star as it rose,[b] and we have come to worship him.” 3 King Herod was deeply disturbed when he heard this, as was everyone in Jerusalem. 4 He called a meeting of the leading priests and teachers of religious law and asked, “Where is the Messiah supposed to be born?”

5 “In Bethlehem in Judea,” they said, “for this is what the prophet wrote:

6 ‘And you, O Bethlehem in the land of Judah,
    are not least among the ruling cities of Judah, for a ruler will come from you
    who will be the shepherd for my people Israel.’”

7 Then Herod called for a private meeting with the wise men, and he learned from them the time when the star first appeared. 8 Then he told them, “Go to Bethlehem and search carefully for the child. And when you find him, come back and tell me so that I can go and worship him, too!” 9 After this interview the wise men went their way. And the star they had seen in the east guided them to Bethlehem. It went ahead of them and stopped over the place where the child was. 10 When they saw the star, they were filled with joy! 11 They entered the house and saw the child with his mother, Mary, and they bowed down and worshiped him. Then they opened their treasure chests and gave him gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh.

12 When it was time to leave, they returned to their own country by another route, for God had warned them in a dream not to return to Herod.

 

            This Epiphany story is truly a story about the star – the light that guided the wise men.  It is a story of the journey of faith, that really began from prophecies of old – like our Scripture today from Isaiah 60, that reads:

 

1“Arise, Jerusalem! Let your light shine for all to see.
    For the glory of the Lord rises to shine on you.
2 Darkness as black as night covers all the nations of the earth,
    but the glory of the Lord rises and appears over you.

3All nations will come to your light;
    mighty kings will come to see your radiance.

 

            Those mighty kings – or wise men – were led by the radiance of a star – and came with many different gifts.   They had the gift of insight and courage to follow their leading.   The gift of perseverance in going on a long journey and faith that following that star would lead them to the newborn king.  

 

            Then there were the gifts that they gave – gold, frankincense, and myrrh – costly gifts that probably helped the young family when they escaped Israel and fled to Egypt to escape the threat on the life of Jesus.   But they also gave the gift of worship and praise to the newborn King.

 

            They had many gifts; they gave many gifts – but first they received many gifts.   They received the gift of illumination and guidance by the brilliance of a star – the gift of insight and warning from the Holy Spirit to go home by a different route – and most importantly the gift of the child, the newborn King!  

 

            Who were these kings?   People led by the Star – filled with many gifts that led them to Christ.   Indeed – so are we!   People who are led by the Star – following it to Christ child.   And what a difference it can make in our lives as we keep our eyes open to see God leading us as we journey through life.

 

            There is a new tradition that many churches around the country are doing on Epiphany Sunday and that is in the receiving of star gifts that might just help us keep our eyes open to see God gifts and His work in our lives throughout the year.  These star gifts are paper stars, with words written on them – simple verbs or adjectives meant to give a point of focus or inspiration for the coming year.    Much like Mary – who kept all the events of the birth of Jesus in heart and pondered them” (Luke 2:19), we will be taking our star word and what it might mean for our faith and life in the new year.

 

            In just a moment, the deacons will be passing out stars to you in our offering plates.   Instead of putting something into it – you will be receiving something from it – a star with a word written on it.   Don’t look first – just reach in and grab!  Try to resist the urge to pick through or to put back or even to trade words – but trust that the right word will find you – that God might just be leading you to that word.

 

            As one pastor put it:

 

            The word that finds you may be a perfect fit.   If so, celebrate it and be intentional about sharing your gift with the world.

 

            The word that finds you may offer encouragement or a challenge you need.   If so, let it shape you in the days, weeks and months to come.

 

            The word that finds you may make you want to resist or even reject it.   If so, prayerfully consider how come and remain open to what this word will teach you.

 

Live with your word, reflect upon it, journal about it, talk about it with others and let me know your stories.  Discover how this Star Word may shine its light on you and your path throughout this coming year.1

 

  Take this star home with you and put it in a place where you will see it regularly: perhaps on your dashboard, or your computer screen or a bathroom mirror.  Allow the word to speak to you in the particular place where you are - in your stage of life – in your faith. 

 

            OK – deacons how about you go ahead and start passing those out?  Now remember, just reach in and grab one and then you can turn it over and see what word it is.  Now, it may be that when you receive that word, you may think, “I can’t imagine how I’m going to see this in my life” – or you may think, “Wow!   I can already see how this applies in my life.” 

 

Think about it this way.   Perhaps the word on your star is a gift you already have but you need to think about it in a new way.   Maybe the word you have represents a gift that is needed by a someone else – a gift that God will uniquely empower you to give.  Or perhaps your word represents a gift you don’t have right now but is one you really need.   What is interesting is to see how God can work through such a simple process and touch our hearts.

 

For example, one woman, from another church, had recently lost her husband.   She received the word “perseverance”.  A student, going off to college, received the word “responsibility”.   A choir director received the word, “Harmony.”

 

One pastor received the word “time” who was getting ready to go on Sabbatical.   Another one received the word “Faith” – which everyone thought was strange, since they assumed their pastor had enough faith.   Little did she know that there would be all sorts of crises that year that would test her faith.   During those moments of trouble and heartache – she would remember that star gift of faith – a gift to hang on to and never to be taken for granted.2

 

My guess is you don’t know right now what the meaning of that word is for you now.   That’s ok.   What I encourage you to do – is not just throw it away – but simply take it home and let it rest with you.   Look up the definition of the word – see how often the word is used in Scripture.  Reflect on what that word could mean for your new year.   Perhaps you will find a new opportunity to give that something to people around you.   Perhaps you will find that receive something new – maybe unanticipated or surprising – something that you cannot attribute to anyone else but God.   And when that happens – however it happens – I hope you will tell us about it, so that we too can catch that glimpse of God at work!

 

We do have plenty of stars – so if you would like to have more than one – you can get another one after worship.   We also have some blank stars if you would like to take another one so that you can write your word on it, and then place your word in a couple of places where you can see it on a regular basis.                                                   

 

            “May these stars be one way to help guide you, as that star did for the wise men long ago, to a place where you discover God breaking into your world in a powerful and personal way.”

 

“Star of wonder, star of night, star with royal beauty bright.   Westward leading, still proceeding, guide us to Thy perfect light.”3

 

PRAYER

            God, we acknowledge that we are not always ready to receive your best gifts for us. You have given us an epiphany word in order that our searching will bring us to you.    Help us to be open to the gift that you offer us now through our star words. We acknowledge that we do not fully understand what this word might mean for our faith, but we receive it from you with gratitude and pray that your Spirit will enable us to live into our word with intention and faithfulness.     Amen.4
