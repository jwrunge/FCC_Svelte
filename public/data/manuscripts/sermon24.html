AT THE CROSSROADS
EASTER SUNDAY – LIFE

 

 

  By Pastor Martha-Jean Fitch

 Sermon Dialogue with Mary Magdalene

 

Drama written by Ted Schroeder. © 2016 Creative Communications for the Parish, www.creativecommunications.com

Edited by Mary Runge
SCRIPTURE:  John 20:1-18

 

 

 

1Early on Sunday morning, while it was still dark, Mary Magdalene came to the tomb and found that the stone had been rolled away from the entrance. 2 She ran and found Simon Peter and the other disciple, the one whom Jesus loved. She said, “They have taken the Lord’s body out of the tomb, and we don’t know where they have put him!”

 

3 Peter and the other disciple started out for the tomb. 4 They were both running, but the other disciple outran Peter and reached the tomb first. 5 He stooped and looked in and saw the linen wrappings lying there, but he didn’t go in. 6 Then Simon Peter arrived and went inside. He also noticed the linen wrappings lying there, 7 while the cloth that had covered Jesus’ head was folded up and lying apart from the other wrappings. 8 Then the disciple who had reached the tomb first also went in, and he saw and believed— 9 for until then they still hadn’t understood the Scriptures that said Jesus must rise from the dead. 10 Then they went home.

 

11 Mary was standing outside the tomb crying, and as she wept, she stooped and looked in. 12 She saw two white-robed angels, one sitting at the head and the other at the foot of the place where the body of Jesus had been lying. 13 “Dear woman, why are you crying?” the angels asked her.

“Because they have taken away my Lord,” she replied, “and I don’t know where they have put him.”

 

14 She turned to leave and saw someone standing there. It was Jesus, but she didn’t recognize him. 15 “Dear woman, why are you crying?” Jesus asked her. “Who are you looking for?”

She thought he was the gardener. “Sir,” she said, “if you have taken him away, tell me where you have put him, and I will go and get him.” 16 “Mary!” Jesus said.

 

She turned to him and cried out, “Rabboni!” (which is Hebrew for “Teacher”).

17 “Don’t cling to me,” Jesus said, “for I haven’t yet ascended to the Father. But go find my brothers and tell them, ‘I am ascending to my Father and your Father, to my God and your God.’”  18 Mary Magdalene found the disciples and told them, “I have seen the Lord!” Then she gave them his message.

 

PASTOR:     

            Crossroads.   That has been the topic we have focused on all throughout the season of Lent.   We all have those times of crossroads.   They come at various times in life and at various stages of life.    And they can cause great disruptions in our lives.   We can feel like we are walking in uncertainty and darkness – anxiety and pain – maybe even despair or depression.

 

            Talk about disruption and crossroads - this past month has been such a time for all of us I think.   We have seen the darkness and suffering and anxiety.  And our lives have been totally disrupted – from having to stay at home, not go to work or restaurants and especially hard, especially on days like Easter – is not going to church.    And all the uncertainty of not knowing when it will all end – and what life will be like after we get to the other side.       

 

         You know that first Easter was really not all that different.  That morning, it was a dark time.   The disciples had seen Jesus, arrested and then beaten and die a horrific death on a cross.  They buried Jesus quickly that Friday, before the Sabbath began – and then huddled in a locked room, afraid of the Jewish leaders.   What would happen now?   Their lives were completely disrupted – and they were devastated.   What would happen now?   They were lost and unsure;  how would life ever be the same?    Some, like Judas, decided to give up.  Others doubted – and some like Peter, I am sure, were filled with guilt and despair.  It’s the same for all of us, isn’t it?   At the crossroads, we can make a choice that follows that line of thinking.  

 

        But there was one – who faced that crossroad on that first Easter Sunday – who even in her grief and sadness – choose a different pathway that led to hope and joy.   Today we welcome Mary Magdalene, a follower of Jesus.  She comes to tell us about the crossroads in her life where she personally met the risen Christ.

 

(Mary enters. She is dressed in biblical costume. She is smiling, joyful)

 

PASTOR:    Welcome, Mary. You are well known to us, but can you tell us a little more about yourself?

 

MARY:          I was, I guess, an early follower of Jesus. I had difficulties throughout my life, especially after the death of my husband.   My life as a widow was hard and even some of my so-called friends turned against me.  They didn’t understand me, and they even spread stories that I was living an immoral life. And the stories not only ruined my reputation but made me an outcast.

 

PASTOR:      What drew you to Jesus?

 

MARY:          I heard him teach and saw his miracles. I heard how he multiplied food to feed thousands. I heard how he had helped the blind to see and even raised some from the dead. I heard him proclaim the good news of the Kingdom of God. But when I first met Jesus, my soul was distraught within me. There was a part of me that was drawn to what he said and proclaimed, but there was another side of me that wanted nothing to do with Jesus.

 

PASTOR:      Sounds like you were at a crossroads from the very first moment you met Jesus. Can you share with us how your relationship with Jesus changed?

 

MARY:          Well, as Jesus drew near to me, he was the only one who could heal my soul. I could not look in his face as I was ashamed of who I was. There was a slight glimmer of hope that maybe he would be the answer to the pain I was experiencing. I was an outcast, a nobody, a widow. And yet Jesus looked upon me and had compassion. He recognized me for who I was and drove out seven demons that I was afflicted by. Finally, I was free. No longer did I see myself as those around me judged me by. I finally saw myself as beloved, healed, and free.

 

PASTOR:      After your healing, you traveled with Jesus as he went from town to town and village to village. You became one of his followers and supported him through his ministry. You were with the others when he was crucified, weren’t you? Didn’t you see him put to death?

 

MARY:          Yes, and at that point my life really did come apart. I had followed him, trusted him and there he was at the mercy of the hated Romans, mocked by the religious leaders. I had so hoped that there would be another way for him to be our savior, another way that he would bring peace to our land.

 

PASTOR:      And did that peace for you ever come?

 

MARY:          Yes, but it was a sense of peace that passed all understanding. As I stood there at the cross-watching Jesus die, I couldn’t help but wonder what might happen next for all of us who were followers of Jesus. As we watched him take his last breath, the pain and grief I was in was great. And yet, there was something within me that held onto hope. Something within me that brought me a sense of peace.  Jesus had taught us that he would come back. I didn’t understand it at the time, but I held onto it. Jesus had brought me back and healed me. If he was the Son of God, surely, he would be able to do what he said.

 

PASTOR: What happened after he died? 

 

MARY:   After the Sabbath was over, I brought spices to anoint Jesus’ body for burial. I was concerned about how I would be able to access Jesus’ body as a guard and a large stone had been put in front of the entrance. But when I arrived, the tomb was empty! I went and told the other disciples and they came to look as well, but we all didn’t know what to make of it. The disciples returned to the house, but I stayed. I couldn’t leave without knowing what happened to Jesus’s body. All I could do was sit there and cry.

  

PASTOR:     And yet you are here sharing joy with us. What changed?

 

MARY:          Here is the miracle, the miracle I would like you to share on this Easter morning. I did not find him. Jesus found me. He came to me, even when I was so broken, I could not recognize him. He came to me, and then spoke my name. And when my name rolled off his lips, everything changed. Everything that he had taught us fell into place. My messiah, my savior whom we had praised as our King to save us was ALIVE

!

PASTOR:      All with the speaking of your name?

 

MARY:          Don’t you see? He knew where I was. And he came to find ME. Of everyone he could have spoken to after he rose from the dead, he came to find me. And by his resurrection, he has made everything new for me — and he will do it for you too.

PASTOR:      Everything.

 

MARY:          At that point I knew I could stop searching for him, for meaning, for all the things I needed to fulfill my life. Then I knew that my life was caught up in him. He made me whole and gave me a challenge.

 

PASTOR:      Challenge?

 

MARY:          I knew that my purpose in life now was to tell what he had done and what he had done for me. Don’t you see? Easter, the resurrection is not just a miraculous event, it is a life-changing crossroad for all of us. In the Risen Christ we are found at all of the stopping places of life, all of the dead ends, and called to new life by the one who purchased and gifted new life for us.

 

PASTOR:      I know that the Risen Christ sent us to teach the new life He has given us. He said: “Go ... and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you.  And behold, I am with you always, to the end of the age.”  (Matthew 28:19-20)

 

Mary: There is, our Easter challenge and promise. He sends even you and me to be his witnesses and He promises to be with us, to keep on being with us, to keep on coming after us, picking us up and sending us again—to the end of the age. That is the hope that we cling to. That is what we can celebrate today. Christ is Risen!

 

PASTOR:      Christ is risen indeed. Amen.

 

            What incredible hope and promise we have in Jesus!  Jesus is alive and He promises to be with us – and will never leave us.  He will be there in all the ups and downs of life and will meet us at each crossroad and give us the help and strength we need right when we need it.   What an incredible message of hope we need for all times – and for our times we are in this day. 

 

            People like Mary Magdalene and so many others have gone before us as witnesses, to spread that good news to each generation.   And we too are called to be witnesses to people all around us. 

 

As we close our message for today, I want to share with you some witness from the spiritual leaders of our church, our elders.   They share how they have felt the power and promise of Easter in their lives and have been changed it.   Let me first share verbally from those who were unable to film their response.   First from Elder Bob Clark:

 

Bob Clark:   When I was pastor at Central Congregational Church – my first Easter was 1991, and we had a 6am Easter Sunrise Service.   There were about 100 people in the pews, which surprised me.    We did the opening call to worship, we sang “Christ the Lord is Risen Today”.    And then I held up my hands to stop everything.    The congregation was facing west, but I was facing east, and I was witnessing the most glorious sunrise I had ever seen, through the stained-glass Rose Window, at the east end of the sanctuary, above the balcony.   You all know that celebrated Rose Window.   I stopped everything and asked the congregation to please turn around.   A unison gasp filled the sanctuary.    Happy Easter!

 

Tina Burns:  Some of my favorite Easter memories are from my youth when we had Sunrise Services.  I remember our Sunday School class putting on a play about the Resurrection story then serving breakfast afterwards. There was just something special about those early morning services.

 

            Carolyn Spratt: Easter starts with the glory of Christmas and Christ being born.  Christ was sent to earth to teach us about God, about how to live as Christians and what we need to believe that we might have that eternal path to heaven.  Through Christ and His death on the cross, God has provided a way for us to be forgiven of our sin.   If we but believe that Jesus is our Lord and Savior and we ask for forgiveness of our sin, it will be given.   Thus, that is the joy of this great Easter morning.   Glory be to God!    Christ is alive.
