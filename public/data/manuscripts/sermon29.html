SHELTER
HANGING ON IN TIMES OF TROUBLE

 by Pastor Martha-Jean Fitch
June 28, 2020
TEXT:   Psalm 138

 

                Have you ever had one of those times when everything just seems to be happening at once?   If it is not one thing, it is another.    Troubles just seem to pile up and you don’t know which way to turn or what to do.

 

            Once a lady wrote a letter to Pastor Lloyd Ogilvie, and said “Lloyd, there is just one word to describe my state of mind when problems begin to pile up – panic!  I can handle them fairly well with the Lord’s help when they come at me one at a time, but when they begin to stack up one on top of the other, I begin to fall apart.”1  

 

            Have you ever felt that way? I’m sure that you have! Almost all of us have had days of anxiety & stress & problems…but somehow it seems right now, they are really stacking up.  What with the coronavirus pandemic, unemployment, economic downturns, civil unrest and injustice, political divisions, and so much bad news!   When you add all that to whatever personal trouble you have like relationship struggles, health concerns, and family worries, it can be overwhelming.   I have heard people share how weary they feel, and how pessimistic they are about what the future holds.   When will everything go back to normal for our nation – for our church – for our family?  Where is God in the midst of these difficult times?   How am I going to hang on in these troubled times?

 

            These kind of questions – and times of trouble – really aren’t that new.   Many generations have struggled and have asked similar questions – and we see that especially in the book of the Psalms that we are focusing on our series called “Shelter”.   Many of the Psalms were written by King David who certainly had his shares of troubles – political unrest, heart-breaking family discord, death of family and deep grief, great fear and even hiding for his life.  

 

We learn two important things from the Psalms – one is that in times of trouble, we can pour out our heart to God.   We can lay out of all thoughts and fears and pain and know that God will hear us.   And secondly, that the way to hang on in times of trouble is to worship and praise God and believe that God is still at work in our lives, even in the midst of our troubles.  We can run to our heavenly Father who is our refuge and shelter.   He will hold us with His strong right hand and will carry us through and work out His purposes in our lives.

 

In Psalm 138, David begins his song by declaring: “Thank you!   Everything within me thanks you!” – “I will thank the Lord with all my heart!”   This is not just a simple note of thanks, this is saying thank you to God with all your soul, mind, and heart.   It is an act of the will and is not based on our feelings or our circumstances.     It is a decision and choice to truly thank God IN everything – including praising Him in the storms of life.   When we choose to hang on to the pain and walk in fear and worry, and refuse to be thankful, for even the hard times, we are keeping part of our hearts from God and we are blocking the strength and the hope that God wants to give us.    What is amazing is that when we focus on praising and thanking God during our times of trouble, we are released from the tensions and pressures of the problem and are more open to receiving His supernatural strength and peace.  David acknowledges this when he says in verse 3: “As soon as I pray, you answer me, you encourage me by giving me strength”.

 

I have discovered this truth throughout my life as I have gone through different trials or struggles.   I have discovered when a problem arises, if I dwell on it – or try to solve it in and of my own understanding and strength, the more weary and weak I become.   But when I start thanking God for being with me in the problems – and trusting He will help me through them, I am given incredible strength and peace…and amazing wisdom and insight to work out and endure whatever problems I am facing.

 

And then I have to remind myself how God has been with me in the past and will surely help me in the future.   David tells us that God is full of unfailing love and faithfulness.   He cares for the humble – and reaches out to the broken-hearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit.   David says in verse 7 that even when we are surrounded by troubles, God is there to help and protect us.

 

  God is indeed greater than all our problems and because of who He is, we have all the help and hope we need!   He holds us in His powerful right hand and saves us from our enemies. 

 

            Mother Teresa was one time asked how she handles the immense problems of Calcutta, Mother Teresa responded, “I focus on the Lord and not the problems.   Then I can deal with the problems holding the strong hand of Jesus.”

 

            We can get through our troubles – clinging to that strong hand of God to save us.

 

            At the end of the psalm, in verse 8, we hear this amazing promise: “The Lord will work out His plans for my life – for your faithful love, endures forever”.   God is still at work in us – to fulfill the plans He has for us.   When we are discouraged or feeling distressed about how life is going, we need to keep on believing that God is still busy at work in us.   God never abandons His plans or His people.   He is at work and is able, more than able to handle anything that comes our way.   He can take all those bruises and scars and pains we go through and will work them together for our good!   Even though we can’t always understand the troubles we are going through – and we don’t always see the plan – we can trust that God holds our future in His hands We can have hope knowing that He will carry us through – through it all.

 

            Back in the 1970’s, songwriter Andrae Crouch was going through a great time of trouble in his life.   He had “experienced devastating loss when he lost his father, mother and brother all within two years of one another.    He said, “Probably the hardest time in my life…just getting through the gloom and grieving part of it and to learn the reason why.”  Later, he was diagnosed and battles four different kinds of cancer and congestive heart failure.  

 

            One day, he was praying and asking the Lord why He took his mother.   He strongly felt God encouraging him to praise Him.   Andrae refused at first saying, “I cannot praise You through this, I cannot do it – I don’t feel like it.”   But after struggling inwardly for a half-hour or so, he finally gave in.   “I just started saying “Thank you Jesus and I praise you Jesus. And then I felt strength come like a gushing well.   The joy of the Lord came in the room and filled my soul.”   Through that experience, Crouch learned that through the pain and trouble of life, there is an incredible power in praising God and learning to trust Jesus. 2

 

            And so, Crouch wrote this song called “Through it All”.   Listen to some of the lyrics:

 

            I’ve had so many tears and sorrows – questions for tomorrow

            There’ve been times I didn’t know right from wrong.

            But in every situation, God gave me blessed consolation

            That all my trials come to only make me strong.

           

            I thank Him for the mountains, and I thank Him for the valleys.

            And I thank Him for the storms, He’s brought me through.

            For if I’d never had a problem, how would I know that He

                        could solve them.

            How would I know what faith in the word of God could do?

 

Chorus:

            Through it all, through it all.

            I’ve learned to trust in Jesus – I’ve learned to trust in God.

Through it all, through it all.  I’ve learned to depend upon His word.

 

I don’t know where you are today.   What struggles are troubling you.   But I can assure you that God knows your heart and holds you in His strong right hand.   I am confident that God will carry you through whatever you are going through as you trust in Him.   I am praying that

You will know that His peace and His strength and His power this day – and every day.
