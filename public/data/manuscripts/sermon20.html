WHO ARE YOU?
SECOND MILE CHRISTIANS

  by Pastor Martha-Jean Fitch
February 16, 2020
TEXT:    Matthew 5:38-42

 

 

(Sermon begins with a 3 ½ minute video depicting a Jewish man and son having to carry a load for Roman soldiers – and them going the second mile.   Can be viewed at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i8DGOcxXA34)

 

            Second Mile Christians.   Followers of Jesus going beyond what is expected!   What an upside down, crazy way to live – but what a difference it can make in the world.

 

            Turn with me to our passage today from the Sermon on the Mount, found in the 5th chapter of the Gospel of Matthew.   In this famous sermon of Jesus – early on in his ministry – he says that we are to live differently than the world.   We are to go above and beyond what is expected and live a Christ-filled life of love and forgiveness.

 

            Look at Matthew 5:38-42.   Jesus says:

 

38 “You have heard the law that says the punishment must match the injury: ‘An eye for an eye, and a tooth for a tooth.’ 39 But I say, do not resist an evil person! If someone slaps you on the right cheek, offer the other cheek also. 40 If you are sued in court and your shirt is taken from you, give your coat, too. 41 If a soldier demands that you carry his gear for a mile, carry it two miles. 42 Give to those who ask, and don’t turn away from those who want to borrow.

43 “You have heard the law that says, ‘Love your neighbor’ and hate your enemy. 44 But I say, love your enemies! Pray for those who persecute you! 45 In that way, you will be acting as true children of your Father in heaven. For he gives his sunlight to both the evil and the good, and he sends rain on the just and the unjust alike. 46 If you love only those who love you, what reward is there for that? Even corrupt tax collectors do that much. 47 If you are kind only to your friends, how are you different from anyone else? Even pagans do that.

 48 But you are to be perfect, even as your Father in heaven is perfect.

 

            Jesus presents us with a new way of living.   Instead of living for ourselves – we are to seek to serve others.   To serve even the ones who hate you and forgive the ones who hurt you.   We are to respond with kindness and go beyond what is expected.   Who are we?  Jesus says we are to be Second Mile Christians.   It is in the second mile living that are most like Jesus and can make a great impact on our world.

 

            But what exactly did Jesus mean when He told us to go the second mile?  Well, in Jesus’ day, the Romans, who had conquered the land, had a practice where they could keep people in their place and knowing who was in charge and it was called the “Roman Mile”.   Roman soldiers could legally coerce Jewish citizens into serving them in whatever capacity or demand that they carry their load for one mile.  These soldiers could call out to a farmer in a field or a merchant from his shop and demand, “You carry my load” – much like you saw depicted on our video.   It was a constant threat the Jews lived under – that a soldier at any time could compel them to do something.   It brought great resentment and anger among the Jews.

  

                We see an example of this in the story of Simon of Cyrene.   Jesus was carrying his cross, through the streets of Jerusalem and He stumbled and fell.   The Romans soldiers called out to Simon of Cyrene and demanded that he come and carry the cross of Jesus. 2  He had to carry it all the way to the edge of Golgotha. 

 

            Now just imagine, people listening to Jesus preach – and He says: “ If anyone forces you to go one mile, go with them two miles”  What in the world?   Was Jesus crazy?   Serving those Gentiles who were their enemies?   What was Jesus was saying?   Max Lucado describes it this way: “Give more than requested.” Go two. At the end of one mile, keep going. Surprise the sandals off the soldier by saying, “I haven’t done enough for you. I’m going a second mile.” Do more than demanded. And do so with joy and grace!”

 

            That first mile is doing what is mandated or what is expected.   But it is in the 2nd mile that you make a choice – to go beyond expectations and show the love of God.   Jesus was calling for people to have a serving attitude that went above and beyond what was required by the law.   The first mile is your duty – the “have to” mile.  But the second mile is a choice – the “want to” mile.3   It comes from a heart that wants to serve others and show God’s love.  It is what sets the followers of Jesus apart and builds up the kingdom of God.  

 

             The first mile is to love people who love us.  That is something, as Jesus says even the corrupt tax collectors can do.    The first mile is to be kind to those who are kind to us….to our friends.   But going beyond to the 2nd mile is to love those who don’t love us – and to be kind to those who are unkind to you.  It’s to pray for those who persecute you – not just for the people that we like.   It is a choice to live differently than the world that emphasizes holding grudges and getting vengeance and hating your enemies.   It is a choice to live like true children of God.

 

             And Second Mile People put others before themselves.   They don’t live life looking out for what is in their own best interests but – are selfless, looking for another’s best interests.   They look to bring out the best in others and lighten the burdens of life.   As Paul said in Philippians 2:  “Be humble, thinking of others as better than yourselves.   Don’t look out only for your own interests, but take an interest in others too.”

 

             In reading about going the second mile this week, I discovered that Dan Cathy, CEO of the Chik-fil-A restaurant, uses “second mile living” as part of his training for his employees.   Cathy says, “Going the second mile means living out the Golden Rule and putting others before ourselves in a world that is all too often about me, me, me”   He instructs his employees to be continually looking out for the customer and how they can be of help.   “The company trains employees to take the trip to the “second mile” by considering that every life has a story and therefore employees should always show compassion and provide superior service.  Everyone we come in contact with throughout our day may be going through some difficulties, and therefore could use a kind smile and a little appreciation.”4

           Thus, Cathy hopes his company will be known for going the second mile.  And it seems to me that’s what we need to be known for as the church.   People who care deeply and go the second mile – to show love and compassion to all.

 

             One way we are doing that is through our ministry of the Blessing Box – going beyond just saying we want to love our neighbors.   We are literally reaching out to help them in a tangible way – giving food to eat and hygiene products and cleaning products. 

 

             Another way we can go the second mile for our neighbors around the world is by our giving to Week of Compassion.   Week of Compassion is the name given to our disaster relief, refugee and development ministry.   For over 70 years, Week of Compassion has been going the extra mile for those in need.   They respond to help people in need, like those who suffered by the recent volcano eruption in the Philippines – or the on-going recovery in Jefferson City, Missouri who experienced tornadoes in 2019 – helping the neighborhoods rebuild. When we give to Week of Compassion, we go the extra mile to help our neighbors in need.   We go the extra mile, because that is what Jesus taught us to do…to help bring God’s kingdom of love and blessings to us and to those we serve.

 

            Second Mile People show Christ-like love and forgiveness – and not just to our neighbors in need – but even to our enemies.  Instead of responding with anger and with evil towards those who have wronged us – we are to go the second mile and show love.  Jesus said if someone strikes you on the right cheek, turn the other cheek.    We aren’t to seek to retaliate and try to get back at people.   Giving an eye for and eye – does nothing but make each other blind!   Nothing is gained by trying to get even… except to bring you down to their level.   You are up here, and they are down here and trying to get even brings you down to their level.  

 

             Second mile people go beyond revenge and follow in the footsteps of Jesus – showing love and forgiveness.   Just as Paul says in Romans 12: “Never pay back evil with more evil.   Do things in such a way that everyone can see you are honorable.   Never take revenge – leave that to the righteous anger of God.   Instead if your enemies are hungry, feed them.   If they are thirsty, give them something to drink.   Don’t let evil conquer you but conquer evil by doing good.”

 

When we respond with good instead of evil – and go that second mile – we show the world the love of God.   People will notice that there is something different about us…much like that soldier did in our movie at the beginning of my message.   What is it that makes you different? You aren’t like the rest of the world.   And that will be our opportunity to share the story of Jesus and His call on our lives to do good and show love and mercy.  You may never know how your living a second mile life can change the environment around you and may even draw someone to Christ.

 

Rev. Stuart Holden was leading some worship services among a group of soldiers in Egypt.   There he saw a really big sergeant who was shining brightly for Jesus.   He was curious and so he asked him, “How were you brought to Christ?”    He said, “There was a private in the same company that I served.   He had converted to Jesus in Malta before the regiment came on to Egypt.   We gave the fellow an awful time.   The devil got possession of me and I made that man’s life a positive burden to him.   Well one night – a terribly wet night -  he  came in from sentry duty.   He was very tired and very wet.   But before getting into bed, he knelt down to pray.   My boots were heavy with wet and mud.  And so, I let him have it on one side of the head and then the other, and he just went on with his prayers.  Next morning, I found those boots beautifully polished by the side of my bed.  That was his reply to me, and it just broke my heart and I was saved that day.”5   That soldier returned good for evil – and witnessed the love of Jesus to his enemy.   He went the second mile!

 

What would that look like for you and for me?   Could we actually do something good for an enemy – for someone who is insulting us and tearing us down?   Could we go beyond expectations – and model God’s love with someone with whom we disagree or have a strained relationship? Could we actually go that second mile and give forgiveness to someone who has wronged us.   I don’t really believe it is possible in some situations – not in our own strength and power.   It is only through the supernatural work of the Holy Spirit working in and through us.   We have to completely surrender our hearts to Him and depend on Him to do the impossible – to actually love our enemies and do good to them. 

 

The reason we want to go the 2nd mile in our lives is because Jesus went the 2nd mile for us!   He knows that road very well!  Oh yes – he walked that first mile – when He walked out of heaven and took on human flesh.   He walked on earth and walked the mandated mile.   But He also went the second mile that was motivated by His great love for you and for me.   That second mile took Him to the cross where He carried the weight – not of a Roman soldier’s backpack – but the weight of all our sins.6   He paid the penalty for our sin – even though He didn’t deserve it.   He went that extra mile for you and for me – and He is asking us to do the same for others.   To love as we have been loved – to be forgiving as we have been forgiven.

 

What will be your answer?   Can you go that second mile?
